Concept discovery in medical VLMs: a rigorous failure.

A faithful re-implementation of the MedConcept pipeline on BiomedCLIP — IU X-Ray (~7k) and ROCOv2 (~80k, 10×). We characterise why SAE concept discovery is non-identifiable, and what a deterministic alternative can do.

Three contributors = three equal parts (5 slides each, ~1 min/slide, ~14 min total). Part 1: problem + setup. Part 2: method + the non-identifiability result. Part 3: deterministic alternatives + evaluation + conclusions.

Part 1 of 3 · Member 1

The problem

Medical VLMs are accurate but opaque.

Strong performance on pathology classification, segmentation and report generation — but the internal representations are high-dimensional, polysemic and unreadable. A serious concern in safety-critical clinical use, where classic post-hoc tools (saliency, attention) rarely resolve into semantic structure.

Ref: main.tex §1 Introduction

Open with: accuracy ≠ understanding. In radiology, a model right-for-the-wrong-reasons is as dangerous as one that is simply wrong.

Saliency/attention show where the model looks, not what it knows in human-readable form. That gap motivates concepts.

Part 1 of 3 · Member 1

Approach

Concept-based XAI: from supervised to unsupervised discovery.

Supervised

Concept Bottleneck Models (Koh 2020) and TCAV (Kim 2018) need a pre-defined, labelled concept set — exactly the limitation unsupervised discovery targets.

Sparse Autoencoders

Bricken et al. (2023): dictionary-learning on activations yields monosemantic latents. The Top-K SAE (Gao 2024) sets L₀ exactly via top-k gating. We adopt it.

MedConcept (Haque 2026)

SAE extraction → alignment to a radiology vocabulary → an LLM-judge with Aligned/Unaligned/Uncertain metrics. The template we re-implement and stress-test.

Impl: src/autoencoder/, src/vocabulary_building/, src/evaluate_llm_judge.py

Two families: supervised concepts (limited by labelled sets) vs unsupervised discovery (decompose + name).

We inherit the MedConcept paradigm (3 stages) but evaluate it rigorously: null-calibrated metrics, permutation-invariant statistics, no un-grounded claims.

SPLiCE (Bhalla 2024) is named here as a deterministic third way — detailed in Part 3.

Part 1 of 3 · Member 1

Backbone & vocabulary

BiomedCLIP (ViT-B/16) + RadLex.

  • BiomedCLIP — ViT-B/16 image encoder + PubMedBERT, pretrained on PMC-15M. Exposes a 768-d hidden state projected by a frozen visual_projection ℝ⁷⁶⁸→ℝ⁵¹² into the shared contrastive space (224×224, L2-normalised).
  • RadLex vocabulary — ~1,030 terms (512-d): 39 anchor queries over 13 sub-domains + 14 NIH ChestX-ray14 seeds, ranked by cosine to a multi-centroid relevance score.
  • Modality gap (Liang 2022) — systematic offset between image and text cones (cross-modal cosine ≈0.27, centroid L2 ≈0.94). Estimated and subtracted before naming/decomposition.

Impl: src/embedding_extraction/extract_embeddings.py, src/vocabulary_building/build_vocabulary.py

Key technical point: 768-d (hidden) → 512-d (contrastive). The baseline operates on the 512-d projected space; Path A goes back to the 768-d pre-projection state.

The modality gap is not a detail — in Part 3 we show correcting it lifts max atom–image cosine 0.49→0.85. It was the binding issue, not the solver.

Data

Two scales: IU X-Ray (1×) and ROCOv2 (10×).

IU X-Ray · 1× · primary

7,470 images · 3,852 studies.

Split by radiographic study (key = patient_IM-study): no study in both train and test. → 5,955 train / 1,515 test (3,081 / 771 studies), verified group overlap = 0, recomputed deterministically.

ROCOv2 · 10× · replication

~80,000 images.

Split by image (no shared patient structure). A 10× replication that lets us test the data-scale hypothesis directly: does more data cure non-identifiability?

Impl: src/utils.py (split_embeddings) · anti-leakage test: tests/unit/test_split.py

Honest methodological choice: IU split by study (no patient leakage), ROCOv2 by image (no shared structure). Deterministic, recomputed each run.

The 10× of ROCOv2 is not “more data = better”: it is the test bed for the scale hypothesis. Spoiler for Part 2: it does not help.

Part 2 of 3 · Member 2

Method

Three paths + an organisation extension.

01 · BASELINE

Top-K SAE 512-d

Faithful MedConcept re-impl. D=2048, k=32, 8k steps, 5 seeds (0, 42, 123, 456, 789). The failure case.

02 · PATH A

Top-K SAE 768-d

CLS hidden state before projection. Bridge with frozen W_proj. Targets Gap 3 (representation site).

03 · PATH B

SPLiCE

Decomposition over a fixed RadLex dictionary: OMP (k=32) + NNLS. Deterministic. Targets Gap 1+4.

04 · ORG

Organisation

Agglomerative clustering + RadLex ancestors with degeneracy guards. Targets Gap 5 (flat concepts).

Impl: src/autoencoder/sae_module.py (SAEManager), src/sae_hidden/, src/concept_discovery/

One SAE config for clean comparisons: D=2048, k=32, 5 seeds. The multi-seed setup is what makes a stability metric possible at all.

Each path has an explicit target gap — nothing is decorative. Path B changes paradigm: a fixed dictionary instead of a learned one.

Stability metric (used from slide 8 on): for each decoder row of seed i, best cosine match across all rows of seed j — permutation-invariant. Null conditioned on the decoder subspace (erank ≈357–363, not 512).

The headline result · Gap 1
1.81×1.67× on IU X-Ray · 1.81× on ROCOv2

The baseline SAE reconstructs faithfully (cosine 0.97–0.99) but its cross-seed best-match is only 1.67×–1.81× the conditioned null. ~0% of features match at ≥0.9; naming capped at 0.40–0.48 (0% above 0.7). Identity is absent — genuine non-identifiability, not a training bug.

Pivot slide. Good reconstruction ≠ canonical features — this is the point to hammer home.

Decoders are full-rank, inits distinct, no collapse → not a bug. It is intrinsic to the representation site (pooled CLS, compressed, contrastive-shaped).

Numbers (Tab.1): matched obs 0.299 (IU) / 0.327 (ROCOv2); subspace null ratio 1.67× / 1.81×; frac ≥0.9 0.0% / 0.3%; naming 0.40 / 0.48.

Relabeling control · Gap 6

The slot-wise metric is floor-by-construction.

Permute one SAE's encoder/decoder rows by a random bijection π — function-preserving, reconstruction unchanged to 10⁻⁴. The slot-wise Jaccard collapses to 0.0077, indistinguishable from real cross-seed. The matched metric correctly reports identity (1.0).

Impl: scripts/relabeling_control.py — relabel_state_dict(sd, perm)

Cross-seed Jaccard matrix (IU X-Ray baseline). Mean ≈0.0077 — the analytical floor. The slot-wise signal is permutation noise, not stability.

Trap experiment: a single SAE, relabelled, is mathematically identical to itself, yet slot-wise Jaccard does not recognise it. So slot-wise is permutation noise, not a stability measure.

This is contribution (1) of the paper. From here, every stability claim uses the matched metric.

The figure (results/iu_xray/figures/failure_jaccard_matrix.png) is generated by scripts/run_failure_case_analysis.py — title “Cross-seed Jaccard (mean …)”.

Path A (768-d) vs baseline (512-d)

Moving the representation site is not enough.

  • Dead features — 16.0% → 1.7% (Path A is healthier)
  • Naming cosine — 0.42 → 0.47
  • Matched obs / null — 1.97× → 2.63× (still weak universality)
  • Frac. matched ≥0.9 — ≈0% → ≈0% (no canonical features)

Impl: src/sae_hidden/ (naming_hidden.py), scripts/run_path_a.py

Naming scores — top-1 feature↔vocab cosine. Most features sit well below 0.5: a name assigned by cosine is cosmetic, not a real semantic match.

Path A is a local improvement (fewer dead features, better naming) but it does not change the global verdict — “weak universality” (Lan 2024, Leask 2025): a shared subspace, not identical features.

Why still not enough: we still operate on the pooled CLS token (the contrastive global summary), not on patch tokens. The paper says this explicitly.

Naming figure (failure_naming_scores.png) titled “Naming scores — X% below 0.5”; matches the naming row 0.42 / 0.47 and the capped 0.40–0.48 claim.

The scale hypothesis · Gap 4
10×ROCOv2 ~80k vs IU X-Ray ~7k

10× more data improves training health (dead features 16%→0.6%) but not identifiability: matched 0.299→0.327, subspace ratio 1.67×→1.81×, frac≥0.9 0%→0.3%, naming 0.40–0.48. The binding constraint is the representation site, not data volume.

Contribution (3): a direct refutation of the “more data = identifiable features” hypothesis.

The 10× moves training-health indicators, not identity ones. End of Part 2: erank ≈357–363 is far below the 2048-way dictionary, so no feature is canonical on such a compressed global vector.

Part 3 of 3 · Member 3

Path B · SPLiCE

Deterministic. Judge-ready.

SPLiCE decomposes each gap-corrected 512-d embedding into a sparse, non-negative combination over the fixed RadLex dictionary: OMP selects k=32 atoms, then NNLS re-solves the coefficients. The dictionary is never estimated → deterministic by construction, zero cross-seed instability.

  • IU X-Ray — 1,515 test in 9.5s · 997/1,031 terms (96.7%) · 14.18 concepts/img.
  • ROCOv2 — 15,958 img in 101s · 1,024/1,024 terms (100%) · 8.56 concepts/img.
  • Gap correction is decisive — max atom–image cosine IU 0.49→0.85, ROCOv2 0.54→0.86.

Impl: src/concept_discovery/spliece.py — decompose_image() · scripts/run_spliece.py

SPLiCE trades an estimation problem (learning the dictionary) for a given one (RadLex fixed). No seed, no non-identifiability.

The output schema is SAE-compatible → the same LLM-judge can score it (matters on slide 13).

Honest caveat: frequent terms are “mixed”, not clean findings (ROCOv2 dominated by imaging modalities; IU has bilingual/off-topic entries). A property of the supplied dictionary, not of SPLiCE.

Part 3 of 3 · Member 3

Faithfulness · Gap 2

A small upper tail is genuinely faithful.

Faithfulness ablation (baseline D=2048, 5 seeds): 17.8%±0.9 of live features exceed the shuffle-null 95th percentile in point-biserial correlation with IU X-Ray MeSH/Problems labels; 1.1% reach |r|>0.30 (strongest |r|=0.459±0.057, on a mass feature).

Yet they stay unstable cross-seed: zero features cluster across the 5 seeds at cosine ≥0.90; only 0.1% in a ≥4-seed cluster at ≥0.70 (not above the shuffle null, p=0.17). Partially faithful, entirely non-reproducible.

Impl: scripts/run_faithfulness.py · main.tex §5.6

Honest, faceted result: a real signal exists (~18% upper tail, and the “mass” feature at |r|=0.459 is clinically plausible), but it is small and unstable.

Not “concepts work” — rather “a weak, non-reproducible tail exists”. Exactly the kind of partially-negative evaluation the paper claims as a contribution.

Part 3 of 3 · Member 3
LLM-as-judge · local plausibility
81.6%vs 3.3% with Llama-3.1-8B

SPLiCE on IU X-Ray: 81.6% Aligned under MedGemma-4B (1,230/1,508) but only 3.3% under Llama-3.1-8B (Uncertain 73.9%). ROCOv2 baseline: 88.3% vs 23.1%. Judge run to convergence (T=0, zero parse errors). Local plausibility orthogonal to global identifiability.

The %Aligned is a property of the judging model as much as of the explanation: the cross-LLM spread (81.6% vs 3.3%) proves it. MedGemma is medical-tuned, Llama is general.

Numbers verified on JSON: results/iu_xray/spliece/judge_scores_*.json (aligned_rate 0.8156 / 0.0325, n=1508) and results/rocov2/baseline/judge_scores_*.json (0.8833 / 0.2314, n=15957).

This does not contradict non-identifiability: a feature can align with one report and still fail to recur across seeds. Two orthogonal axes.

Part 3 of 3 · Member 3

Conclusions

Three lessons that generalise.

Null + permutation-invariant

Interpretability claims about discovered concepts must be calibrated against analytical nulls and a permutation-invariant statistic. Slot-wise overlap is degenerate.

Scale is the last suspect

For a failing factorisation, data scale is the least likely culprit: 10× changed training health, not identifiability.

Partially-negative evaluation

An honest, partially-negative evaluation is more useful than a curated one. The defect is localised (representation site), not global.

Ref: main.tex §6 — 4 contributions: relabeling control · matched metric + null · scale refutation · partially-negative eval

Synthesis for the committee: we did not “break” the pipeline — we showed where and why SAE concepts are not identifiable, and localised the constraint in the representation site.

SPLiCE is the only deterministic, judge-ready alternative; ~18% upper tail is faithful; organisation helps but is weakly separated.

Limitations & future work

SAE on the patch-token residual stream.

The diagnosis is predictive: decomposing BiomedCLIP patch-token residual stream (a mid layer, not the pooled CLS) should restore identifiability — the experiment our characterisation points to as the next step.
